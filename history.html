Pre-historic Era :

      Java has been inhabited by their ancestors (hominina) since prehistoric times. Around 40,000 years ago, Aboriginals and Melanesians colonised Central Java. They were assimilated or replaced by Mongoloid Austronesians by about 3,000 BC, who brought with them technologies of pottery, the bow and arrow, and introduced domesticated pigs, fowls, and dogs.

Hindu-Buddhist and Islamic Era :

      Hinduism and Buddhism, were brought by the Indians from South Asia, at the time of Central Java was a centre of power in Java back then.The first dated inscription in Central Java was the Canggal from 732 AD (or 654 Saka). In the late 16th century, the development of Islam had surpassed Hinduism and Buddhism as the dominant religion in Java. The emergence of the Islamic kingdoms in Java is also inseparable from the role of Walisongo.

Dutch Colonial Rule :

      By the late 16th century, European traders began to frequent central Javanese ports. The Dutch established a presence in the region through their East India Company. During the Napoleonic Wars in Europe, Central Java as a Dutch colony was handed over to the British. However, the Dutch also brought modernisation to Central Java. In the 1900s, the predecessor of the modern Central Java was created, named Gouvernement of Midden-Java.

Independence and Contemporary Era :

      On 1 March 1942, the Imperial Japanese Army landed on Java, and the following week, the Dutch colonial government surrendered unconditionally to Japan. Following the surrender of Japan, Indonesia proclaimed its independence on 17 August 1945.
